# Historical Graphic & Art Movements

*28 styles in this family (9 flagship). Generated from `scripts/style_catalog.json` — the single source of truth.*

Movements from art and design history — Bauhaus, Swiss, De Stijl, Art Deco, Memphis, Pop Art and more. A client asking for one of these wants the UI to *evoke an era*, so accuracy to the movement's actual principles (grid logic, palette, type, motif) matters more than any modern UI convention.

> **How to apply this family.** **Translate the movement's principles into UI, don't just paste a motif.** Bauhaus is a grid and primary triad, not a clip-art circle; Swiss is a mathematical grid, not just Helvetica. Research the movement's real rules and let them drive layout, type, and color.

### Art Deco  ⭐ *flagship — full spec in [00-flagship-implementation-specs/art-deco.md](00-flagship-implementation-specs/art-deco.md)*
*Aliases:* Deco, Style Moderne  
*Slug:* `art-deco` · *Era:* 1920s–1930s

**Origin.** Paris 1925 Exposition Internationale des Arts Décoratifs.

**Defining traits.**
- Symmetry, geometric and stepped (ziggurat) forms
- Luxurious gold/black/jewel tones
- Sunburst, chevron, zigzag motifs
- Elegant high-contrast display type

**Reference example.** Chrysler Building; The Great Gatsby posters; Cassandre travel posters.

**Don't confuse with:** art-nouveau, streamline-moderne, bauhaus.

### Bauhaus  ⭐ *flagship — full spec in [00-flagship-implementation-specs/bauhaus.md](00-flagship-implementation-specs/bauhaus.md)*
*Aliases:* Bauhaus style  
*Slug:* `bauhaus` · *Era:* 1919–1933

**Origin.** German Bauhaus school (Walter Gropius, Weimar/Dessau).

**Defining traits.**
- Form follows function; geometric primitives (circle, square, triangle)
- Primary red/yellow/blue plus black & white
- Sans-serif geometric type (Futura lineage)
- Asymmetric balance, no ornament

**Reference example.** Herbert Bayer's Universal typeface; Bauhaus exhibition posters.

**Don't confuse with:** de-stijl, constructivism, swiss-design.

### Constructivism  ⭐ *flagship — full spec in [00-flagship-implementation-specs/constructivism.md](00-flagship-implementation-specs/constructivism.md)*
*Aliases:* Russian Constructivism, Soviet design  
*Slug:* `constructivism` · *Era:* 1915–1930s

**Origin.** Russia/USSR (Alexander Rodchenko, El Lissitzky, Stenberg brothers).

**Defining traits.**
- Bold diagonals and dynamic asymmetry
- Red, black, cream; heavy sans/slab type
- Photomontage, geometric shapes as propaganda
- Industrial, purposeful, revolutionary

**Reference example.** Rodchenko posters; El Lissitzky 'Beat the Whites with the Red Wedge'.

**Don't confuse with:** suprematism, bauhaus, futurism-italian.

### De Stijl / Neoplasticism  ⭐ *flagship — full spec in [00-flagship-implementation-specs/de-stijl.md](00-flagship-implementation-specs/de-stijl.md)*
*Aliases:* Neoplasticism, Mondrian style  
*Slug:* `de-stijl` · *Era:* 1917–1931

**Origin.** Netherlands (Theo van Doesburg, Piet Mondrian).

**Defining traits.**
- Only horizontals and verticals; thick black rules
- Primary colors + white/gray/black
- Asymmetric rectangular compositions

**Reference example.** Mondrian 'Composition' paintings; Rietveld Schröder House.

**Don't confuse with:** bauhaus, constructivism.

### Fauvism  ⭐ *flagship — full spec in [00-flagship-implementation-specs/fauvism.md](00-flagship-implementation-specs/fauvism.md)*
*Aliases:* Les Fauves, wild beasts style  
*Slug:* `fauvism` · *Era:* 1904–1908

**Origin.** Paris (Henri Matisse, Andre Derain, Maurice de Vlaminck); named after a critic called the painters 'les fauves' (wild beasts).

**Defining traits.**
- Wild, non-naturalistic, highly saturated clashing color
- Bold flat unmixed color fields applied straight from the tube
- Expressive, loose, brush-like edges and borders
- High-energy arbitrary palette: hot orange, cobalt, viridian, magenta

**Reference example.** Matisse 'Woman with a Hat' (1905); Derain's Collioure harbor paintings.

**Don't confuse with:** pop-art, psychedelic, memphis-design.

### Sumi-e (Japanese Ink Wash)  ⭐ *flagship — full spec in [00-flagship-implementation-specs/sumi-e.md](00-flagship-implementation-specs/sumi-e.md)*
*Aliases:* ink wash painting, suibokuga, brush ink style  
*Slug:* `sumi-e` · *Era:* 14th century–present

**Origin.** Traditional East Asian ink wash painting, brought to Japan via Zen Buddhist monks from Song-dynasty China.

**Defining traits.**
- Monochrome black ink gradients (bokashi) on cream rice-paper backgrounds
- Visible brush-stroke texture and ink-bleed edges
- Deliberate negative space (ma) as a compositional element
- Asymmetric, organic compositions with a single red seal (hanko) accent

**Reference example.** Sesshu Toyo's landscape scrolls; Hasegawa Tohaku 'Pine Trees' screen.

**Don't confuse with:** ukiyo-e, japandi, wabi-sabi.

### Swiss / International Typographic Style  ⭐ *flagship — full spec in [00-flagship-implementation-specs/swiss-design.md](00-flagship-implementation-specs/swiss-design.md)*
*Aliases:* International Style, Swiss Style, grid design  
*Slug:* `swiss-design` · *Era:* 1950s–1960s

**Origin.** Switzerland (Josef Müller-Brockmann, Armin Hofmann); Basel & Zurich schools.

**Defining traits.**
- Mathematical grid systems and rigorous alignment
- Helvetica/Akzidenz-Grotesk, flush-left ragged-right
- Objective, neutral, hierarchy via size/weight
- Generous whitespace, photography over illustration

**Reference example.** Müller-Brockmann concert posters; Helvetica.

**Don't confuse with:** bauhaus, minimalism, flat-design.

### Art Nouveau
*Aliases:* Jugendstil, Liberty style  
*Slug:* `art-nouveau` · *Era:* 1890–1910

**Origin.** Europe (Alphonse Mucha, Hector Guimard, Gustav Klimt).

**Defining traits.**
- Organic, sinuous 'whiplash' curves from nature
- Floral and botanical ornament, flowing hair
- Muted earthy + gold palettes
- Decorative custom lettering

**Reference example.** Mucha's theatrical posters; Paris Métro entrances.

**Don't confuse with:** art-deco, solarpunk.

### Blackletter / Gothic Type
*Aliases:* Fraktur, Old English, gothic script  
*Slug:* `blackletter` · *Era:* 1150–1500 (revived)

**Origin.** Medieval European calligraphy; Gutenberg's type.

**Defining traits.**
- Dense angular condensed letterforms
- Ornate capitals, heavy vertical strokes
- Newspaper mastheads, tattoo, metal-band use

**Don't confuse with:** goth, illuminated-manuscript.

### Op Art

*Slug:* `op-art` · *Era:* 1960s

**Origin.** Bridget Riley, Victor Vasarely.

**Defining traits.**
- Black-and-white (or vibrating color) geometric patterns
- Illusions of motion, depth, and warping
- Precise repetition of lines/shapes

**Defining traits.**

### Suprematism
*Aliases:* Malevich style  
*Slug:* `suprematism` · *Era:* 1913–1920s

**Origin.** Russia (Kazimir Malevich).

**Defining traits.**
- Pure geometric abstraction floating in space
- Limited palette, often on white ground
- Feeling over representation
- Circles, rectangles, crosses, tilted planes

**Reference example.** Malevich 'Black Square'; 'Suprematist Composition'.

**Don't confuse with:** constructivism, de-stijl.

### Ukiyo-e

*Slug:* `ukiyo-e` · *Era:* 1603–1868

**Origin.** Edo-period Japan (Hokusai, Hiroshige).

**Defining traits.**

### Victorian
*Aliases:* ornate Victorian, 1800s ornament  
*Slug:* `victorian` · *Era:* 1837–1901

**Origin.** Victorian-era Britain and US.

**Defining traits.**
- Dense ornamentation, filigree, flourishes
- Multiple decorative typefaces per page
- Gold, deep jewel tones, engravings
- Circus/advertising exuberance

**Reference example.** Victorian trade cards; circus posters.

**Don't confuse with:** arts-and-crafts, steampunk.

### Vienna Secession
*Aliases:* Secessionist, Wiener Werkstätte  
*Slug:* `vienna-secession` · *Era:* 1897–1905

**Origin.** Vienna (Gustav Klimt, Koloman Moser, Josef Hoffmann).

**Defining traits.**
- Geometric grids of squares married to ornament
- Gold leaf, decorative symbolism
- Custom geometric lettering
- Bridge between Art Nouveau and Deco

**Reference example.** Klimt Beethoven Frieze; Ver Sacrum journal.

**Don't confuse with:** art-nouveau, art-deco.

### WPA / Vintage Poster
*Aliases:* travel poster, screenprint poster, WPA  
*Slug:* `wpa-poster` · *Era:* 1935–1943

**Origin.** US Works Progress Administration Federal Art Project.

**Defining traits.**
- Flat screen-printed color areas
- Bold simplified shapes and shadows
- Limited ink palette, gradient skies
- Optimistic public-service messaging

**Reference example.** WPA national park posters; vintage airline/travel posters.

**Don't confuse with:** mid-century-modern, risograph.
