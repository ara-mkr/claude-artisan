# Minimal / Maximal / Organic / Layout-Driven

*15 styles in this family (5 flagship). Generated from `scripts/style_catalog.json` — the single source of truth.*

Styles defined by *how much* and *how arranged* rather than by a surface material: minimalism and maximalism at the extremes, bento grids and broken grids as layout systems, organic/blob and Scandinavian/Japandi as softness-and-restraint philosophies.

> **How to apply this family.** **These are composition disciplines — spacing, hierarchy, and restraint carry them.** Minimalism fails when whitespace is timid; maximalism fails when the chaos has no anchor. Get the type scale and spacing scale right before adding anything decorative.

### Bento Grid  ⭐ *flagship — full spec in [00-flagship-implementation-specs/bento-grid.md](00-flagship-implementation-specs/bento-grid.md)*
*Aliases:* bento box layout, bento UI  
*Slug:* `bento-grid` · *Era:* 2022–present

**Origin.** Named after Japanese bento boxes; popularized by Apple keynote slides & MagicUI.

**Defining traits.**
- Modular rounded-rectangle cells of varied sizes
- Each cell one idea/feature; dense but tidy
- Consistent gaps, mixed media per cell
- Scannable dashboard-like composition

**Reference example.** Apple keynote feature slides; Vercel/Linear marketing; MagicUI.

**Don't confuse with:** minimalism, dashboard-analytics.

### Maximalism  ⭐ *flagship — full spec in [00-flagship-implementation-specs/maximalism.md](00-flagship-implementation-specs/maximalism.md)*
*Aliases:* more is more, maximal  
*Slug:* `maximalism` · *Era:* 2018–present

**Origin.** Reaction against flat minimalism; 'more is more'.

**Defining traits.**
- Dense layering, clashing color and pattern
- Big expressive type, many focal points
- Ornament, texture, collage, saturation
- Bold, joyful visual overload

**Reference example.** Gucci editorial; many 2020s portfolio/agency sites.

**Don't confuse with:** memphis-design, neubrutalism.

### Minimalism  ⭐ *flagship — full spec in [00-flagship-implementation-specs/minimalism.md](00-flagship-implementation-specs/minimalism.md)*
*Aliases:* minimal, less is more  
*Slug:* `minimalism` · *Era:* 1960s art → 2000s digital

**Origin.** Roots in minimal art (Donald Judd) + Dieter Rams; digital via Apple/Google.

**Defining traits.**
- Extreme restraint: whitespace, few elements
- Limited (often monochrome) palette
- Strong typographic hierarchy carries design
- Every element earns its place

**Reference example.** Apple product pages; Dieter Rams Braun; Google homepage.

**Don't confuse with:** swiss-design, flat-design, minimalism-japanese.

### Dark Mode
*Aliases:* dark theme, night mode  
*Slug:* `dark-mode` · *Era:* 2018–present

**Origin.** Mainstreamed by iOS/Android/macOS dark themes (2018–2019).

**Defining traits.**
- Dark neutral backgrounds (#0d0d12-ish, not pure black)
- Desaturated accent colors to avoid vibration
- Elevation via lighter surfaces, not shadow
- Reduced eye strain, OLED-friendly

**Reference example.** System dark modes; Linear; developer tools.

**Don't confuse with:** minimalism, bento-grid.

### Japandi
*Aliases:* Japanese Scandinavian  
*Slug:* `japandi` · *Era:* 2017–present

**Origin.** Hybrid of Japanese wabi-sabi and Scandinavian minimalism.

**Defining traits.**
- Muted earthy neutrals, natural materials
- Calm, warm, functional restraint
- Handcraft imperfection (wabi-sabi)

**Reference example.** Muji-adjacent interiors; premium wellness brands.

**Don't confuse with:** scandinavian, zen-wabisabi, minimalism.
