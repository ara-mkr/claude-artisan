# Texture / Material / Rendering Techniques

*29 styles in this family (4 flagship). Generated from `scripts/style_catalog.json` — the single source of truth.*

Surface treatments that can be a full aesthetic or an accent layer: holographic, duotone, halftone, risograph, glitch, ASCII/CRT, isometric, low-poly, pixel, grain. Many are print or display artifacts turned into deliberate style.

> **How to apply this family.** **Decide up front: full treatment or accent?** A halftone hero is different from a halftone everywhere. Textures read as intentional when they're systematized (one grain, one dot size, one glitch behavior) and cheap when sprinkled randomly. Keep text on a clean layer.

### ASCII / Terminal / TUI  ⭐ *flagship — full spec in [00-flagship-implementation-specs/ascii-terminal.md](00-flagship-implementation-specs/ascii-terminal.md)*
*Aliases:* terminal UI, TUI, ANSI art, green screen  
*Slug:* `ascii-terminal` · *Era:* 1970s–present

**Origin.** Text-only terminals; revived as retro/hacker aesthetic.

**Defining traits.**
- Monospace text-only layouts, box-drawing chars
- Green/amber phosphor on black
- Blinking cursor, ASCII art, command-line framing
- Utilitarian, hacker, nostalgic

**Reference example.** Old DOS/Unix terminals; modern TUIs; hacker movie UIs.
